BookWave Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 1, 2026

Last updated: June 5, 2026

BookWave is an iOS app for reading EPUB books, listening with text-to-speech, and tracking reading and listening progress. This Privacy Policy explains how BookWave handles information.

Information BookWave Stores

BookWave may store information needed for app functionality, including your imported books, library metadata, reading progress, highlights, bookmarks, listening activity, app settings, and reading or listening statistics.

BookWave stores data on your device. When you are signed in to iCloud, library metadata such as book titles, folder organization, reading progress, highlights, and bookmarks may sync through Apple's CloudKit service in your private iCloud account. Imported EPUB files may sync through iCloud Drive in BookWave's app container. If iCloud is unavailable, BookWave may keep some data only on your device until iCloud becomes available again.

BookWave does not operate its own servers for storing your library, imported books, highlights, bookmarks, reading progress, or listening statistics.

Imported Books and User Content

BookWave allows you to import EPUB files and create reading-related data such as highlights, bookmarks, and progress records. Your imported books and reading data are used only to provide app features such as reading, text-to-speech, progress tracking, highlights, bookmarks, and library storage.

Imported EPUB files may be stored in BookWave's iCloud Drive container when iCloud Drive is available. If iCloud Drive is unavailable, BookWave may store imported books locally on your device.

BookWave does not provide, sell, verify, or authorize the books or files you import into the app. BookWave does not sell your imported books, reading activity, highlights, bookmarks, or personal reading data.

Highlights, Bookmarks, and Statistics

BookWave may store highlights, bookmarks, and reading progress. Highlights may include the text you selected. When iCloud is available, highlights, bookmarks, and reading progress may sync across your devices through CloudKit as part of your library metadata.

BookWave may also store reading and listening statistics on your device, such as daily reading or listening time and estimated words read or listened to. These statistics are used for in-app statistics features and are not synced across devices as part of the library sync described above.

Subscriptions and Purchases

BookWave may offer auto-renewable subscriptions through Apple In-App Purchase. Payments, renewals, cancellations, refunds, and subscription management are handled by Apple.

BookWave uses Apple StoreKit to load products, process purchases, restore purchases, and check subscription entitlement status.

BookWave does not use a third-party subscription analytics or entitlement service.

Analytics and Tracking

BookWave does not include third-party advertising SDKs, third-party analytics SDKs, or ad tracking.

BookWave does not sell personal data.

Text-to-Speech

BookWave uses system text-to-speech functionality to read supported book text aloud. BookWave does not send your book text or generated audio to BookWave servers for text-to-speech processing.

Apple Services

BookWave may rely on Apple services such as iCloud, CloudKit, iCloud Drive, StoreKit, and system text-to-speech. Your use of Apple services is also subject to Apple's privacy policies and your Apple account settings.

External Links

BookWave may include links to external pages such as privacy, terms, support, or Apple subscription management resources. If you open an external link, your use of that website or service is subject to its own privacy policy and terms.

Children's Privacy

BookWave is not intended to knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe that a child has provided personal information through the app, please contact us.

Data Deletion

You can delete imported books, highlights, bookmarks, and app data from within the app where supported. BookWave also provides an option to delete synced library data, which removes synced library metadata from CloudKit and imported EPUB files from BookWave's iCloud Drive container for your Apple ID, including on your other devices when sync completes.

You may also remove app data from your device or iCloud using iOS and iCloud settings.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.
